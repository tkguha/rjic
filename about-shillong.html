About Shillong

The Scotland of the East

Nestled amidst the cloud-kissed peaks of the Meghalayan paradise, Shillong weaves a spellbinding tapestry of enchantment. As the capital of the state of Meghalaya and a sanctuary of ethereal beauty, it sits gracefully at an elevation of 4,908 feet, embraced by the majestic Umiam gorge, and the watchful Diengiei Hills.

Once the revered haven of the mighty deity U Shyllong, whose guardianship blessed the city from the revered Shillong Peak, this historic marvel was born as the new civil station for the Khasi and Jaintia Hills in the late 19th century. Before 1972, it proudly held the mantle of the British-created Assam's capital, exuding a heritage that breathes life into every street and corner.

Often hailed as the 'Scotland of the East,' Shillong graciously mirrors the emerald grandeur of the Scottish highlands, adorned with a verdant tapestry of pine conifers, the fragrant allure of pineapple shrubs, and an orchestra of trekking trails and natural sanctuaries. In the beating heart of this cultural haven, the rhythms of the Khasi Khynriam, Khasi Pnar and Garo tribes resonate, painting a vibrant tableau of tradition and indigenous splendor.

In the embrace of the twilight, Shillong transforms into the pulsating 'Rock Capital of India,' where the city's nightlife reverberates with the spirit of exhilarating rock music, casting a spell that bewitches every soul that steps foot on its sacred soil.

Travel Tips for Visitors

Best Time to Visit

The ideal time to visit Shillong is between October and April when the weather remains pleasant and scenic views are at their best.

Local Transport

Taxis and shared cabs are widely available. Walking is also enjoyable in many scenic parts of the city.

Weather Essentials

Carry light jackets or sweaters even in summer. During monsoon months, umbrellas and raincoats are essential.
